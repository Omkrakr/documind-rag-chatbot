Remote Work Policy

Employees may work remotely up to three days per week, subject to manager approval. Full-time remote arrangements require written approval from both the manager and HR business partner, and are reviewed every six months.

All remote employees must be reachable during core hours, 10 AM to 4 PM local time, and must attend mandatory in-person events such as quarterly town halls and annual planning sessions.

Equipment Policy

The company provides a laptop, monitor, and basic peripherals for remote work. Employees may request a one-time home-office stipend of up to twenty thousand rupees within their first ninety days of a remote arrangement. Stipend requests are submitted through the internal expense portal and require manager sign-off.

Leave Policy

Employees accrue 1.5 days of paid leave per month, capped at 18 days annually. Unused leave up to 10 days may be carried forward to the next calendar year; anything beyond that is forfeited unless local law requires payout. Sick leave is tracked separately and does not require advance notice, though a doctor's note is required for absences longer than three consecutive days.

Maternity leave is 26 weeks paid, and paternity leave is 2 weeks paid, both available regardless of tenure. Adoption leave mirrors maternity leave for the primary caregiver.

Performance Review Cycle

Formal performance reviews happen twice a year, in April and October. Managers and employees both complete a self-assessment and peer feedback is collected from at least two colleagues. Compensation changes resulting from reviews are typically reflected in the following month's payroll.

Probation Period

New hires are on a 90-day probation period during which either party may end employment with two weeks' notice. Probation can be extended once, by up to 30 days, if a manager flags performance concerns in writing before the original 90-day mark.
